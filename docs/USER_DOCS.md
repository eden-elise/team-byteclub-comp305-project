# BYTECLUB – User Documentation

## 1. Overview

**BYTECLUB** is a turn-based roguelike adventure set in **Castle Nocturne**, a mysterious vampire castle. Players wake up
with no memory of how they arrived and must climb through five floors filled with enemies, puzzles, and story events.

**Key features:**
- **Roguelike gameplay:** Each run is unique due to procedural generation. When your character dies, you start over.
- **Turn-based combat:** Make strategic decisions against enemies without being rushed.
- **Text-driven exploration:** Move through rooms, make choices, and interact with your environment through text options.
- **Visual enhancements:** Simple pixel art and lightly animated backgrounds accompany the story.

---

## 2. Game Setup

### 2.1 Starting a Game
1. Open BYTECLUB in your web browser.
2. **Select a character:** Each class has randomized starting stats (HP, Attack, Defense, Speed).
3. Your character begins on **Floor 1** in a dungeon cell.

### 2.2 Controls
- Navigate rooms using text options provided on screen.
- Combat and decisions are turn-based; click or select actions when prompted.
- Actions may include:
    - Attack
    - Use a special potion
    - Use an item

---

## 3. Gameplay Mechanics

### 3.1 Movement
- The game is **grid-based**; you move from room to room.
- Entering a room reveals its description, enemies, or items.
- Some rooms contain items, clues, or more.

### 3.2 Combat
- Combat alternates turn-by-turn between the player and enemy.
- Each turn, you can:
    - **Attack** (physical or magical)
    - **Use an item** (heal, buff, debuff)
- Defeating enemies can drop loot, increase stats, or unlock memory fragments.

### 3.3 Items and Inventory
- Items include potions, stat boosts, or weapons.
- Some items have **special effects**, like granting an extra turn or debuffing an enemy.
- Inventory management is key; using items strategically increases survival chances.

### 3.4 Memory Fragments
- Found throughout floors in rooms or after defeating certain enemies.
- Unlock narrative details and small gameplay bonuses.
- Example: discovering a note might give context to a recurring enemy.

---

## 4. Floors and Story Progression

### 4.1 Floor 1 – Awakening Cells
- **Environment:** Prison cells, chains, cold stone
- **Story beat:** You wake up with no memory; find a ripped note: *“He erased your past. He always does.”*
- **Goal:** Learn basics, combat, and item use.

### 4.3 Floor 2 – Study & Archives
- **Environment:** Dusty library, lab tables, locked archives
- **Enemies:** Cursed Scholar
- **Story beat:** A journal in your handwriting warns, *“If he finds me again, he will wipe my mind clean.”*

### 4.4 Floor 3 – Grand Hall & Private Quarters
- **Environment:** Banner-filled hall, old bedroom, private corridors
- **Enemies:** Memory Wraith
- **Story beat:** You realize this is not your first escape attempt; your memory is being reset.

### 4.5 Floor 4 – Tower Stairs & Battlements
- **Environment:** Spiral stairs, crumbling stone, sunrise horizon
- **Enemies:** Lord Dravik (Final Boss)
- **Ending Choices:**
    - **Defeat Dravik** → Escape & break the memory loop
    - **Join Dravik** → Become his apprentice again; game resets

---

## 5. Tips for Players
- **Explore every room:** Items, clues can be missed, and more.
- **Use items wisely:** Combat becomes progressively harder on higher floors.
- **Pay attention to story hints:** Small text changes often indicate important memory fragments.
- **Learn enemy patterns:** Turn-based combat allows you to plan moves strategically.

---

## 6. Saving and Progression
- **Save system:** Encode your current run into a string and upload it later to continue.
- **Permadeath:** Once your character dies, that run ends. Progress is mainly through memory fragments collected.

---

## 7. Visual and Audio Style
- **Art:** Pixelated characters, simple backgrounds for each floor.
- **UI:** Text boxes display story, options, and combat actions.
- **Audio:** Minimal, ambient music or sound effects for immersion.

---

## 8. Target Audience
- Fans of **roguelikes**, text-based games, and vampire/castle-themed stories.
- Players who enjoy **strategic decision-making** in turn-based combat.
- Gamers looking for **short runs (5–10 minutes)** with story rewards.

---

## 9. Known Risks and Considerations
- Game is primarily **text-driven**; visuals are supportive.
- Choices influence stats and story text but **do not open alternative routes**.
- Some randomness in procedural generation may affect difficulty or loot.
